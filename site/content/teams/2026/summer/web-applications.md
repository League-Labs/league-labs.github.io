The Summer 2026 Web Applications team builds the web tooling that supports League Labs projects and communication.

Areas of work:

* small internal tools for signups, schedules, or coordination
* dashboards and project status pages
* documentation sites and team resources
* frontend and backend workflows for student-built apps

Suggested outputs:

* a working web app or prototype
* deployment notes and contributor instructions
* reusable UI or content patterns for future cohorts

## Project Ideas

Students are welcome to propose their own projects, but here are a few starter ideas to think about. Pick one to join, or use them as inspiration for a pitch of your own. Each has a longer write-up linked below.

* [**League Pods**](../web-projects-ideas/league-pods/) — a platform for parents to form small pods around a class or after-school program, recruit other families, host the activity at someone's home or church, and book the vendor.
* [**Summer Camp Jam**](../web-projects-ideas/summer-camp-jam/) — help parents coordinate a summer of camps with their friends: scrape camp listings, line up schedules, find the overlap between friend groups, and share carpools.
* [**Sublet for Good / Civic Space**](../web-projects-ideas/sublet-for-good/) — match nonprofits and schools with vacant commercial space that landlords aren't filling, including optional furniture rental and a volunteer-keyholder model.
* [**Science at Home**](../web-projects-ideas/science-at-home/) — a marketplace for short science events: birthday-party demos, robotics sessions, kitchen-chemistry visits. Families book providers; providers list their kits.
* [**The Quartermaster**](../web-projects-ideas/quartermaster/) — an inventory and lending system for tabletop gamers: catalog your minis, terrain, and books; lend and trade with friends; never lose track of who has the dragon.
* [**Link Up**](../web-projects-ideas/link-up/) — a group scheduler for squads and friend groups. Merges everyone's calendars with AI-captured preferences ("I hate mornings, never weekends, practice Tues/Thurs nights") and proposes the times most people can actually pull up.

See the [project ideas section](../web-projects-ideas/) for the full write-ups.
